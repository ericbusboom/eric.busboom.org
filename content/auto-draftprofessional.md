## What I Do

I am an entrepreneur, philanthropist, technologist and data analyst, focusing on how to make data easier for nonprofits, journalists, governments and socially oriented companies to use. I run a company, [Civic Knowledge](<http://civicknowledge.com/>), have a non profit project, [The San Diego Regional Data Library](<http://sandiegodata.org/>), and build open source software, [Metatab.](<http://metatab.org/>) I’m currently the board  President at [The League of Amazing Programmers](<http://jointheleague.org/>), and was formally the founding board President at [San Diego Canyonlands](<http://www.sdcanyonlands.org/>).

## My Work

My passion project is the [San Diego Regional Data Library](<https://www.sandiegodata.org>), where I run socially significant data projects, such as: 

  * [Developing a data set of homeless sleeper counts](<http://downtown-homelessness.sandiegodata.org/>) for the Downtown San Diego Partnership, analyzing the data, and helping [students](<https://www.sandiegodata.org/2019/06/homeless-in-the-gaslamp-district/>) and [citizens](<https://www.sandiegodata.org/2019/07/data-for-social-good-workshop-homelessness-and-urban-planning/>) learn how to analyze it themselves. 
  * Building and maintaining a [large collection of public data](<https://data.sandiegodata.org/>) related to San Diego County, some of which, such as [our crime datasets](<https://data.sandiegodata.org/?s=crime>), are frequently used in research. 
  * [Providing research data collections](<https://data.sandiegodata.org/collections/california-department-of-education/>) to the San Diego Hunger Coalition to support their free meals advocacy programs. 


I [work with commercial organizations](<https://www.civicknowledge.com/>) to :

  * Build data oriented websites.
  * Provide data analysis training and support.
  * Improve and maintain data pipelines.


I'm also an Open Source programmer with extensive contributions including:

  * [Metatab](<https://metatab.org>), a data packaging system.
  * [Libical](<https://libical.github.io/libical/>), the most popular implementation of the Internet protocol for exchanging calendar invitations.
